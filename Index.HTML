Mini
Marketing Audit

Most business owners don't realize their website is quietly leaking leads. Sometimes it's the copy. Sometimes it's the structure. Sometimes it's just hard to use.

This quick checklist gives you a fast, honest look at what might be slowing your growth — and what to fix first.

1

Section 01

Messaging: Are You Saying the Right Thing?

The average visitor decides in seconds whether they care.

Can a stranger tell what you do within 5 seconds?
Are you talking about their problems — or just listing your services?
Does the tone sound like a real person or corporate jargon?
Is it obvious why someone should trust or choose you?

If this part's unclear, no fancy design will save it.

2

Section 02

CTAs: Are You Guiding People or Leaving Them Hanging?

If your site doesn't make it obvious what to do next, people leave.

Do you have one clear next step (book, call, quote, etc.)?
Is that CTA easy to find and consistent across pages?
Do your buttons say things like "Get Started" instead of "Submit"?
Does every page have a clear point, or is it just a wall of info?

Less friction = more action.

3

Section 03

Proof: Why Should They Believe You?

Even if you say all the right things, people need to see it to believe it.

Do you have real testimonials that speak to results or outcomes?
Are you showing any proof — before/afters, stats, recognitions, logos?
Does your design feel modern, confident, and trustworthy?
Is there a human face anywhere — you, your team, your clients?

Social proof isn't just for big brands — it's your best shortcut to trust.

4

Section 04

Mobile & Usability: Is It Easy to Use?

A ton of people will only see your site on their phone. That experience matters.

Does your site load quickly (under 3 seconds is the goal)?
Can you scroll easily and read everything on mobile?
Are buttons big enough to tap with your thumb?
Is your contact form short and simple?

If it feels clunky, people bounce.

What Now?

If you nailed most of this — awesome. If a few things stood out as weak spots, that's good too. It means there's opportunity.
